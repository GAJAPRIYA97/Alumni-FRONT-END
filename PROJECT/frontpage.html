Holy Cross College (Autonomous)

In keeping with its mission, the College admits a number of students from the socially and economically weaker strata of the society, irrespective of religion and caste. The history of Holy Cross College dates back to 1902, when Mother Sophie a French Missionary set up a primary school with only a room or two and 5 girls. From its humble beginning, it has steadily grown, attained the status of a college in 1923, became a Second Grade College in 1928 and Post Graduate College in 1964.
